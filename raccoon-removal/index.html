Signs You Might Need Raccoon Removal Services and Common Problems

Recognizing the signs of a raccoon infestation early can save you significant time, money, and stress. Raccoons are nocturnal creatures, so you may not see them directly, but their presence is often evident through the damage and messes they leave behind. Understanding these indicators can prompt you to seek professional help before the situation escalates.

Noises in Attics or Walls

Hearing scratching, scurrying, or thumping noises in your attic, walls, or chimney, especially at night, is a very common sign of raccoon activity. Females seeking a safe place to raise their young are often attracted to these warm, sheltered areas. These noises can be unsettling and indicate a potential den site. Prompt action is needed to prevent damage to insulation and wiring.

Damaged Trash Cans and Scattered Garbage

Raccoons are notorious for raiding trash cans in search of food. If you find your trash cans overturned, lids or handles damaged, and garbage scattered around your property, it's a strong indication that raccoons are frequent visitors. Securing your trash cans can help deter them, but removal may be necessary if they persist.

Pet Food Disappearance or Disturbed Pet Areas

Leaving pet food outdoors, even for a short period, can attract raccoons and other wildlife. If you notice pet food missing or disturbed feeding areas, raccoons might be the culprits. They are opportunistic eaters and will take advantage of easily accessible food sources.

Damaged Roofs, Fascia, or Vents

Raccoons possess impressive dexterity and strength, allowing them to tear at roofing materials, fascia boards, and vents to gain access to the inside of your home. Look for torn shingles, damaged soffits, or bent or missing vent covers. These are clear signs of attempted or successful entry by raccoons or other wildlife like squirrels. We offer expert squirrel removal as well.

Presence of Droppings

Raccoon droppings are tubular and often contain undigested food particles. They are typically found in specific locations called latrines, such as on decks, patios, or in attics. Raccoon feces can contain harmful parasites and bacteria, making it important to handle cleanup cautiously and call a professional for significant infestations.

Unpleasant Odors

The presence of raccoon urine and feces can create strong, unpleasant odors that permeate your home, especially if they have established a den in an interior space like an attic. These odors are not only off-putting but can also indicate a health risk due to the potential presence of pathogens.

Visible Raccoon Sightings

While primarily nocturnal, raccoons can sometimes be seen during the day, particularly if they are hungry or their den has been disturbed. Seeing a raccoon on your property during daylight hours, especially if it appears disoriented or aggressive, warrants caution and professional assessment. It's important to maintain a safe distance and avoid direct contact.

Understanding Raccoon Behavior and Habitat

Raccoons are highly adaptable creatures, which contributes to their ability to thrive in urban and suburban environments like Casas Adobes. Understanding their natural behavior and habitat preferences can shed light on why they might be attracted to your property. They are naturally found in wooded areas near water sources but readily adapt to living near humans, where food and shelter are often abundant.

Adaptable Foragers

Raccoons are omnivores with a diverse diet. They will eat almost anything, including fruits, nuts, insects, small animals, and of course, human garbage. Their foraging instincts drive them to seek out easily accessible food sources, making unsecured trash or outdoor pet food particularly appealing. This adaptability in their diet makes them persistent in their search for food.

Excellent Climbers and Swimmers

Raccoons are skilled climbers, using their sharp claws and agile bodies to scale trees, fences, and even the sides of buildings. This allows them to access attics, roofs, and other elevated areas. They are also strong swimmers, although they typically avoid water unless necessary. Their climbing abilities are a primary factor in their ability to enter homes.

Nocturnal Activity

As primarily nocturnal animals, raccoons are most active during the night. This is why you often hear them scratching or moving around in your attic after dark. Their nocturnal nature can make spotting them during the day less common, but their daytime presence can indicate illness or desperation for food.

Seeking Shelter and Den Sites

Female raccoons, especially, seek out safe and secluded locations to build their dens and raise their young. Attics, chimneys, crawl spaces, and even spaces under decks or sheds offer ideal refuge from predators and the elements. These den sites become focal points of activity and can lead to significant damage and sanitation issues within your home. Dealing with wildlife like raccoons requires specialized knowledge, just like effective ant control requires understanding ant colony structures.

Potential Risks Associated with Raccoon Infestations

Beyond the immediate nuisance and damage, raccoon infestations pose several health risks and other dangers to humans and pets. Being aware of these risks underscores the importance of prompt and professional raccoon removal.

Disease Transmission

Raccoons can carry various diseases and parasites that can be transmitted to humans and pets. These include rabies, a viral disease that is almost always fatal if left untreated; raccoon roundworm, a parasite found in their feces that can cause severe neurological damage in humans; and leptospirosis, a bacterial disease that can cause flu-like symptoms. Handling raccoon droppings or having direct contact with a sick raccoon poses a significant health risk. We also provide bed bug extermination to address other types of pests that pose health concerns.

Property Damage

Raccoons can cause substantial damage to your property. Their efforts to enter homes can result in torn siding, damaged roofing, and compromised insulation. Once inside, they can chew on electrical wiring, posing a fire hazard, and contaminate insulation with urine and feces, requiring costly replacement. They can also damage ductwork and cause structural issues.

Aggression and Bites

While not inherently aggressive, raccoons can become defensive if they feel threatened, especially when protecting their young. A cornered or sick raccoon may bite or scratch, potentially transmitting diseases. It is crucial to avoid direct confrontation with raccoons.

Attraction of Other Pests

Raccoon droppings and leftover food can attract other pests, such as insects and rodents, creating a cascading pest problem on your property. Addressing the raccoon issue and cleaning up the mess they leave assists in preventing future pest infestations, including effective rodent control measures.

Preventative Measures and Long-Term Solutions

Once the raccoons have been removed and your property is secured, implementing preventative measures is essential to avoid future infestations. Our team can provide recommendations and assistance with these long-term solutions.

Securing Trash Cans and Outdoor Food Sources

This is a fundamental step in preventing raccoons from being attracted to your property. Use sturdy trash cans with tight-fitting lids, and consider securing them with bungee cords or placing them in a secured shed or garage. Avoid leaving pet food or other edibles outdoors overnight. For other outdoor pest issues, consider our mosquito control services.

Trimming Trees and Branches

Overhanging branches can serve as bridges for raccoons to access your roof and other parts of your house. Regularly trimming trees and branches away from your roof can reduce their ability to climb onto your home. This simple maintenance can significantly impact preventing wildlife access.

Repairing and Sealing Entry Points

Following our initial exclusion work, periodically inspect your home for any new potential entry points. Even small cracks or gaps can be exploited by determined wildlife. Promptly repair any damage to roofing, siding, vents, or chimneys. This ongoing maintenance is crucial for long-term prevention.

Installing Chimney Caps and Vent Covers

Uncovered chimneys and vents are inviting access points for raccoons and other wildlife. Installing sturdy chimney caps and vent covers with mesh can effectively prevent entry. Ensure that the mesh is small enough to exclude raccoons but still allows for proper ventilation.

Securing Pet Doors

Pet doors, while convenient, can also be used by raccoons to enter your home, especially if the door is not properly secured or is large enough for a raccoon to squeeze through. Consider using electronic pet doors that only open for your pet or ensure the door is locked when not in use.

Removing Potential Den Sites

Areas under decks, sheds, or porches can become attractive den sites for raccoons. Consider enclosing these areas with lattice or other durable materials to prevent access. Removing piles of debris or woodpiles near your home can also reduce potential nesting locations. Just like dealing with raccoon dens, addressing termite infestations requires attention to their nesting and feeding areas.
